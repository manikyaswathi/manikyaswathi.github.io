The International Supercomputing Conference (ISC HPC) 2025, held June 10–13 at the Congress Center Hamburg, marked the **40th anniversary** of one of the world's premier events in high-performance computing, AI, data analytics, and quantum computing. With over 3,500 attendees and a theme of "Connecting the Dots," the conference brought together global researchers, engineers, and policymakers united around the future of intelligent, sustainable computing.

---

## ICICLE @ ISC: Posters, Talks, and Smart Infrastructure

Set against the backdrop of Hamburg — Germany's maritime gateway, home to the iconic Elbphilharmonie concert hall and the UNESCO-listed Speicherstadt warehouse district — the conference offered not just technical depth but cultural inspiration.

I had the pleasure of presenting ICICLE research across three tracks — **Women in HPC**, **Research Posters**, and **Project Showcases** — in collaboration with Dr. Karen Tomko:

- **WHPC Poster:** *Motivated by Challenges: Harnessing AI to Revolutionize Resource Management and Workflow Management in High-Performance Computing*
- **Research Poster:** *Intelligence Plane: A Framework for Machine Learning Application Life-cycle Management*
- **Project Poster:** *Hybrid Black-Box and White-Box Approaches for Efficient Resource Prediction for AI Workloads in High-Performance Computing*

Each poster was accompanied by a lightning talk sharing ICICLE's contributions to AI-driven resource scheduling and runtime optimization across edge-to-HPC environments.

## Spotlight: Responsible AI and Agriculture

Having attended the ICICLE Seminar featuring **Dr. Manish Parashar**, I was deeply inspired by his talk on *"Harnessing Responsible AI for Science Discovery, Innovation, and Impact."* His emphasis on democratizing AI and building inclusive, transdisciplinary cyberinfrastructure resonated with ICICLE's mission — and it was exciting to hear his perspective again at ISC HPC 2025 during the BoF session *"Agriculture Empowered by Supercomputing."*

The discussion focused on how HPC, AI, and distributed systems can enhance resilience through yield prediction, supply chain optimization, and precision breeding — while bridging the gap between edge devices and national supercomputing infrastructure.

## A Panel That Hit Close to Home

One session that stood out was *"Is HPC Energy Efficiency All About Hardware?"* — a theme that directly complements our ICICLE research. The panel posed critical questions: Is energy optimization solely a hardware challenge, or must we adopt software-driven strategies for smarter, more sustainable computing?

These are precisely the questions we confront developing **AI-driven edge model planners** and **energy-aware schedulers**. It was validating to see global researchers — hardware architects, software developers, and AI engineers — united around the same goal.

## Workshops & Tutorials: Personal Favorites

The final day of ISC is always my favorite — dedicated to hands-on workshops with smaller, focused groups. Highlights:

- **"First International Workshop on Foundational Large Language Model Advances for HPC"** — exploring how LLMs can support code generation, compiler optimization, and parallel performance tuning.
- **"Leveraging and Evaluating LLMs for HPC Research"** — practical insights into tailoring LLMs for scientific workflows, bridging usability, performance, and scalability.
- **"Flux: Next-Generation Workload Management for HPC and Cloud"** — Flux's graph-based architecture and database-driven scheduling resonated strongly with our Smart Scheduler work under ICICLE's Ai4CI effort.

## Final Thoughts

ISC HPC 2025 was more than a conference — it was a dynamic convergence of vision, innovation, and purpose. From agricultural resilience to energy-efficient AI, and from LLMs in science to the evolution of workload scheduling, every session helped bridge disciplines, technologies, and communities. I left Hamburg more confident than ever that collaborative, responsible design — across software, hardware, and societal systems — is what drives impactful innovation.
